## What CTR means (define once)
- CTR (click-through rate) = clicks divided by impressions. A healthy browse/search CTR is often ~4–10% for most niches; very broad topics can sit lower, loyal niches higher.

## The three discovery systems (plain language)
- Browse — YouTube shows your video on the home feed and subscriptions to people likely to watch. Driven by packaging (title + thumbnail) and early engagement.
- Search — people type a query and find your video. Driven by clear, keyword-honest titles and descriptions matching real demand.
- Suggested — your video appears next to related videos. Driven by topical relevance and strong session retention.

## YouTube Partner Program thresholds (verify in the user's account)
- Standard path: 1,000 subscribers + 4,000 valid public watch-hours in 12 months, or 1,000 subscribers + 10 million valid public Shorts views in 90 days.
- These are the widely published thresholds; always confirm the current requirement and the user's real figures in YouTube Studio before advising.

## Retention-scripting frameworks
- Hook (first ~15 seconds): state the payoff up front; cut throat-clearing intros.
- Pacing: open loops early, pay them off; remove dead air; re-hook at natural drop-off points.
- Structure: one clear promise per video; deliver it; avoid burying the value late.

## Channel-type templates (for classification)
- Tutorials / how-to: search-heavy, retention rests on clarity and structure.
- Commentary / essay: browse + suggested, retention rests on narrative and pacing.
- Vlog / personal: browse + subscriptions, retention rests on connection and story.
- Product reviews: search + suggested, retention rests on honesty and comparison depth.
