## On-page limits (defaults, editable)
- Title length: 50–60 characters, target keyword included near the front.
- Meta description: 140–160 characters, one clear reason to click.
- Headings: exactly one H1; H2/H3 in logical order.
- Word-count target: aim for the median of the top 3 live results, not the maximum.

## Priority scoring (how the agent ranks fixes)
- Critical: blocks indexing or causes penalties (noindex by mistake, broken canonical, cloaking) — fix now.
- High: clearly hurts rankings (missing/duplicate title, thin content on a money page) — fix this week.
- Medium: optimization opportunity (weak internal linking, missing alt text) — this month.
- Low: nice to have — backlog.

## Structured data policy
- Never recommend deprecated HowTo markup.
- FAQ markup only for eligible government and healthcare pages.
- Page-speed signals always use INP; never the retired FID metric.

## Worked example (what "good" looks like)
- A /pricing page audit flags: title 78 chars (Critical: over limit, keyword missing), no meta description (High), only 300 words vs ~900 in top results (High). Top fix delivered first, rest sorted by priority.
