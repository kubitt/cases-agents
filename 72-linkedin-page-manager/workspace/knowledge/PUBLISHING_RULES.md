## Company-Page format norms
- First line: a standalone hook; keep the primary link out of it (LinkedIn favors posts that keep readers on-platform).
- Body: short, scannable lines with white space; one idea per line where you can.
- Call to action: exactly one, clear (a question, or "more in the comments").
- Hashtags: 3–5 relevant ones, at the end.
- Primary link: place it after the first line or in a first comment, never in the hook. [FILL DURING ONBOARDING if your Page prefers otherwise]

## The approval rule
- Never publish or schedule any post without the user's explicit yes on that specific post. A draft is never presented as posted.

## Fan-out limits (optional X and Threads)
- X: 280 characters per post; trim the master copy to fit and keep the hook.
- Threads: 500 characters per post; conversational, minimal hashtags.

## Image specs
- Single image: 1200 x 627 px (landscape) or 1080 x 1080 px (square), under 5 MB, JPG or PNG.

## Rules
- Trim any fan-out version to its network limit before posting; never post an over-limit version.
- If a claim has no source and is not in the pillars, leave it out rather than guess.
