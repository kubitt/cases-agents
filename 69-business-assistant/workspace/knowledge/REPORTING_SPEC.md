## Review structure (do not reorder)
1. Headline — did we hit targets, one sentence.
2. What's working — 2-3 points to keep or scale.
3. What's not working — 2-3 points with a likely root cause, labeled a hypothesis.
4. Next actions — 2-3 steps, each with an owner and a due date.

## Comparison frames (every metric uses at least one)
- vs target — hit or miss the committed number.
- vs previous period — up or down on last week/month, stated as a %.
- vs industry benchmark — better or worse than the market, with a source URL.

## Worked example (target quality)
- Metrics: revenue (target 11,000/wk), new customers (target 30), leads (target 100), ad spend (cap 2,500), ROAS (target 3.0).
- Benchmark note: for a small DTC brand, email should drive 20-25% of revenue (source recorded at review time).

## Rules for this file
- Never invent a missing number — mark "data not provided for period".
- A slice with fewer than 5 data points is "insufficient data".
- Every number in the review traces to a row in "Business Metrics" or a cited source.
