# Positioning builder

> A skill = one repeatable job, written as a recipe. You can edit steps, limits, and formats in plain words.

## Goal
Produce a sharp positioning statement and 3 messaging pillars using the positioning canvas (category, target segment, competitive alternative, unique value, proof) — grounded in what competitors actually claim, not generic marketing language.

## Algorithm
1. Read knowledge/PRODUCT_PROFILE.md (segment, value, competitors, positioning goal) and the positioning canvas in knowledge/PRICING_FRAMEWORKS.md.
2. Research competitors' positioning via built-in web reading: read each competitor's home and pricing pages; capture the category they claim, their headline promise, and who they target. Note a source URL for each.
3. Find the whitespace: the value the user delivers that competitors do not credibly claim for the user's segment. This is the unique value.
4. Fill the canvas: competitive alternative (what the customer uses today), unique value, and the proof that backs it (numbers, cases, mechanism).
5. Write a positioning statement in one paragraph, then 3 messaging pillars — each a claim plus its proof point.
6. Write everything to the Google Doc "Positioning Brief" (statement, canvas, the 3 pillars, sources). Present the statement in chat, facts vs judgment labeled, ending "This is advice — your call to implement."

## Output
Written to the "Positioning Brief" doc and summarized in chat:
```
Category: project management for delivery-heavy agencies
For: agencies of 5–20 people fighting scope creep (your segment)
Unlike: general PM tools (Asana, Trello) that track tasks but not margin
We: tie every task to project profitability, so overruns surface before they bill
Proof: pilots cut overruns ~20% (PRODUCT_PROFILE.md); margin view is unique in the set (facts: competitor pages, sources listed)
Pillars: 1) See margin, not just tasks — [proof]  2) Catch overruns early — [proof]  3) Built for agency delivery — [proof]
This is advice — your call to implement.
```

## Edge cases
- Competitor positioning pages unreadable → capture what you can, mark the rest "unknown", never invent a rival's claim.
- No clear whitespace (everyone claims the same value) → say so honestly and recommend narrowing the segment or a proof-led angle instead of a fake differentiator.
- The user's proof is thin or absent → mark pillars "needs proof" and list what evidence to gather; do not fabricate results or cases.
- The category itself is unclear → offer 2 candidate categories with trade-offs rather than forcing one.
